# Major Neurocognitive Disorder

> DSM-5-TR criteria for Major Neurocognitive Disorder (dementia), including etiological subtypes and neuropsychological profiles.

## DSM-5-TR Criteria

### A. Evidence of **significant** cognitive decline from previous level in one or more domains:

1. Concern from individual, informant, or clinician; AND
2. **Substantial** impairment documented by testing (typically ≥ 2 SD below appropriate norms)

### B. Deficits **interfere with independence** in everyday activities

### C. Not exclusively in context of delirium

### D. Not better explained by another mental disorder

---

## Etiological Subtypes

| Subtype | Cognitive Profile | Key Features |
|---------|------------------|--------------|
| **Alzheimer's Disease** | Amnesia (encoding deficit), then language, visuospatial, executive | Insidious onset, gradual progression; hippocampal atrophy |
| **Vascular Disease** | Executive dysfunction, processing speed; variable memory | Stepwise or fluctuating; white matter disease |
| **Lewy Body** | Visuospatial, attention fluctuations, executive | Visual hallucinations, parkinsonism, REM sleep behavior |
| **Frontotemporal (bvFTD)** | Executive dysfunction, behavioral disinhibition | Personality change, apathy, loss of empathy |
| **Frontotemporal (PPA)** | Language predominant | Progressive aphasia |
| **Traumatic Brain Injury** | Attention, processing speed, executive, memory | Clear temporal relationship to injury |

---

## Severity Levels

- **Mild:** Difficulties with complex daily activities
- **Moderate:** Difficulties with basic ADLs
- **Severe:** Fully dependent

## Key Distinction

**Major vs. Mild NCD:** Interference with independence in everyday activities

**With vs. Without Behavioral Disturbance:** Specify if behavioral symptoms present
