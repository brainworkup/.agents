# HIPAA Compliance in Neuropsychological Practice

> HIPAA requirements specific to neuropsychological reports and practice, including the 18 identifiers, de-identification methods, minimum necessary standard, and release of information.

## 18 HIPAA Identifiers

Must be removed for de-identified reports:

1. Names
2. Geographic subdivisions smaller than state
3. Dates (except year)
4. Telephone numbers
5. Fax numbers
6. Email addresses
7. Social Security numbers
8. Medical record numbers
9. Health plan beneficiary numbers
10. Account numbers
11. Certificate/license numbers
12. Vehicle identifiers
13. Device identifiers
14. Web URLs
15. IP addresses
16. Biometric identifiers
17. Full-face photographs
18. Any other unique identifying code

---

## De-identification Methods

**Safe Harbor:** Remove all 18 identifiers. Ages >89 become "90+." Dates keep year only.

**Special Neuropsych Concerns:**
- Detailed injury narratives may be identifying
- Rare diagnoses increase re-identification risk
- School names, employer names, clinician names are indirect identifiers

---

## Minimum Necessary Standard

| Referral Type | What to Include |
|---------------|----------------|
| Treatment | Full report typically appropriate |
| School accommodations | Cognitive profile, functional impact, recommendations |
| Employer/disability | Functional capacity; limit irrelevant personal history |
| Legal/forensic | Scope defined by psycholegal question |

---

## Release of Information

Requires patient authorization specifying:
- What information is released
- Who releases it and who receives it
- Purpose of disclosure
- Expiration date
- Right to revoke

**Special:** Separate authorization may be needed for raw test data vs. report
