# Postconcussional Syndrome / Persistent Post-Concussive Symptoms

> ICD-10 F07.81 criteria for postconcussional syndrome, including common symptom clusters and neuropsychological considerations for evaluation of persistent symptoms following mild traumatic brain injury.

## Symptom Clusters

| Domain | Common Symptoms |
|--------|----------------|
| Cognitive | Attention, memory, processing speed deficits |
| Physical | Headache, dizziness, fatigue, photophobia, phonophobia |
| Emotional | Irritability, anxiety, depression, emotional lability |
| Sleep | Insomnia, hypersomnia |

---

## Clinical Course

- **Most concussions resolve within 1–3 months**
- Persistent symptoms (>3 months) warrant comprehensive evaluation
- Risk factors for persistence: prior concussions, psychiatric history, migraines, litigation

---

## Assessment Considerations

1. **Differentiate** cognitive deficits from emotional/psychiatric contributions
2. **PVT/SVT is essential** — high base rates of invalid performance in compensation-seeking contexts
3. **Consider pre-injury factors:** prior concussions, psychiatric history, learning disabilities, ADHD
4. **Litigation/secondary gain** requires heightened validity assessment
5. **Symptom validity** matters as much as performance validity

---

## ICD-10 Coding

- **F07.81** — Postconcussional syndrome
- DSM-5-TR: See Mild NCD due to TBI

---

## Key Differential

- Symptoms may be entirely psychological (depression, anxiety, somatization)
- Some patients have genuine cognitive impairment
- Most have a mix of neurological and psychological factors
- The evaluator's job is to weigh evidence for each contribution
