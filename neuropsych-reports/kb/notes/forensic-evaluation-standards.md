## Role Clarification

| Role | Primary Duty |
|------|-------------|
| Treating clinician | Duty to patient |
| Independent examiner | No treatment relationship; retained by one party |
| Court-appointed expert | Neutral; appointed by the court |

## Admissibility Standards

**Daubert Standard (federal and most states):**
1. Is the theory/technique testable/falsifiable?
2. Has it been peer-reviewed and published?
3. What is the known error rate?
4. Are there standards controlling the technique?
5. General acceptance in the scientific community?

**Frye Standard (some states):**
- Is the methodology generally accepted by the relevant community?

**Implications:**
- Use validated, peer-reviewed tests with established norms
- Apply published cutoffs for validity measures
- Base conclusions on established diagnostic criteria (DSM-5-TR)
- Cite supporting literature; acknowledge limitations

---

## Malingering Assessment

In forensic contexts, explicitly address:
- PVT and SVT results
- Consistency with known neurocognitive patterns
- Consistency of symptoms with test performance
- Standard cutoff compliance
- Base rates in clinical vs. litigating populations

---

## Third-Party Observers

- **APA and NAN position:** Observers (including attorneys) should NOT be present during testing
- Observation alters test performance and invalidates norms
- If court-mandated, document potential impact on validity
- Audio/video recording has similar concerns
