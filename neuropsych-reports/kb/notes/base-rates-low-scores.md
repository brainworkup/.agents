# Base Rates of Low Scores in Healthy Populations

> Understanding how many low scores are expected in a comprehensive battery of healthy individuals is essential to avoid over-diagnosing impairment from normal statistical variation.

## Expected Low Scores (≤ 5th Percentile)

| Battery Length | Expected # of Scores ≤ 5th %ile |
|---------------|--------------------------------|
| 5 tests | 0–1 |
| 10 tests | 0–2 |
| 15 tests | 1–3 |
| 20 tests | 1–4 |
| 25 tests | 2–5 |
| 30+ tests | 2–6 |

**Key reference:** Binder, Iverson, & Brooks (2009)

---

## Clinical Implications

1. **A single low score ≠ impairment** — may be normal variability
2. **Look for convergent evidence:** multiple low scores within the same cognitive domain
3. **Consider pattern:** scattered low scores across domains vs. clustered low scores
4. **Domain consistency matters more than isolated scores**
5. **Larger batteries = more expected low scores** (multiplicity problem)

---

## Interpretive Framework

| Finding | Interpretation |
|---------|---------------|
| 1 low score in 20-test battery | Likely normal variation |
| 2–3 low scores in same domain | Possible domain-specific weakness |
| 4+ low scores across domains | Consider global decline or invalidity |
| No low scores despite complaints | Consider PVT/SVT, symptom validity |

---

## Key Point

The more tests you administer, the more likely you are to find at least one "impaired" score by chance alone. Always interpret individual scores within the context of the full battery and clinical presentation.
