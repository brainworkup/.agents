# Premorbid Estimation

> Methods for estimating a patient's cognitive ability level before injury or illness. Critical baseline for determining whether current performance represents decline.

## Reading-Based Methods (Preferred)

| Test | Ages | Approach |
|------|------|----------|
| Test of Premorbid Functioning (TOPF) | 16–90 | Word pronunciation; co-normed with WAIS-IV/WMS-IV |
| Wechsler Test of Adult Reading (WTAR) | 16–89 | Word pronunciation |
| WRAT-5 Reading subtest | 5–85+ | Word reading used for estimation |

**Rationale:** Reading ability is relatively resistant to most acquired brain injuries and dementias (early stages).

---

## Demographic Methods

| Method | Variables Used |
|--------|---------------|
| Barona estimate | Age, education, occupation, region, sex |
| Oklahoma Premorbid Intelligence Estimate | Education, occupation, age |

**Limitations:** Wide confidence intervals (±15–20 points); less precise than reading-based methods.

---

## Best Practice

1. **Use multiple methods** — reading test + demographic estimate + historical data
2. **Compare to current scores** — FSIQ, GAI, or domain-specific indices
3. **Consider premorbid history** — education quality, occupational complexity, developmental history
4. **Report confidence** — premorbid estimates have inherent uncertainty; note range
5. **Be cautious with:** aphasia, dyslexia, low education, English-as-second-language

---

## Hold vs. Don't Hold Tests

| "Hold" Tests (Resistant) | "Don't Hold" Tests (Sensitive) |
|--------------------------|-------------------------------|
| Vocabulary | Block Design |
| Information | Digit Symbol / Coding |
| Word Reading (TOPF/WTAR) | Letter-Number Sequencing |
| Matrix Reasoning (sometimes) | Processing Speed |

Discrepancies between hold and don't hold suggest acquired decline.
