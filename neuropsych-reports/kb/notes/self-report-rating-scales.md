# Self-Report Rating Scale Interpretation

> Interpretive ranges for commonly used self-report screening measures for depression, anxiety, and PTSD in neuropsychological evaluation.

## BDI-II (Beck Depression Inventory)

| Score | Severity |
|-------|----------|
| 0–13 | Minimal |
| 14–19 | Mild |
| 20–28 | Moderate |
| 29–63 | Severe |

---

## BAI (Beck Anxiety Inventory)

| Score | Severity |
|-------|----------|
| 0–7 | Minimal |
| 8–15 | Mild |
| 16–25 | Moderate |
| 26–63 | Severe |

---

## PHQ-9 (Depression)

| Score | Severity |
|-------|----------|
| 0–4 | Minimal/None |
| 5–9 | Mild |
| 10–14 | Moderate |
| 15–19 | Moderately Severe |
| 20–27 | Severe |

---

## GAD-7 (Anxiety)

| Score | Severity |
|-------|----------|
| 0–4 | Minimal |
| 5–9 | Mild |
| 10–14 | Moderate |
| 15–21 | Severe |

---

## PCL-5 (PTSD)

| Score | Interpretation |
|-------|---------------|
| 0–30 | Below threshold |
| 31–33 | Provisional PTSD diagnosis cutoff |
| ≥ 33 | Probable PTSD |

---

## Clinical Integration

- These are **screening tools**, not diagnostic instruments
- Always interpret in context of clinical interview
- Consider medical conditions that may inflate scores (TBI, chronic pain, fatigue)
- Compare subjective complaints to objective cognitive performance
- Document in Emotional/Behavioral section of report
