# Specific Learning Disorder (SLD) Criteria

> DSM-5-TR diagnostic criteria for Specific Learning Disorder, covering reading (dyslexia), written expression, and mathematics (dyscalculia). Includes neuropsychological considerations for assessment.

## DSM-5-TR Criteria

**A.** Difficulties learning academic skills persisting ≥ 6 months despite targeted intervention (≥ 1 of):
1. Inaccurate or slow/effortful word reading
2. Difficulty understanding meaning of what is read
3. Difficulties with spelling
4. Difficulties with written expression
5. Difficulties mastering number sense, facts, or calculation
6. Difficulties with mathematical reasoning

**B.** Affected skills substantially and quantifiably **below age expectations**, causing significant functional interference

**C.** Learning difficulties began during school-age years

**D.** Not better accounted for by ID, sensory deficits, other disorders, psychosocial adversity, lack of instruction, or language proficiency

## Neuropsychological Approach

- **Achievement-IQ discrepancy model** is no longer required
- **Pattern of Strengths and Weaknesses (PSW)** approach is commonly used
- Identify cognitive processing weaknesses underlying academic deficits
- Phonological processing deficits are hallmark of dyslexia
- Rule out ADHD, anxiety, depression, sensory deficits as primary cause
- Use cross-battery assessment (CHC theory) for comprehensive evaluation
