## Metric Definitions

| Metric | Mean | SD | Used For |
|--------|------|----|----------|
| Standard Score | 100 | 15 | WAIS, WISC, WMS, WIAT, achievement tests |
| Scaled Score | 10 | 3 | WAIS/WISC subtests, WMS subtests |
| T-Score | 50 | 10 | TMT, Stroop, BNT, rating scales, MMPI, PAI |
| z-Score | 0 | 1 | Research, PASAT, SDMT |
| Percentile | 50 | — | Universal metric; percentage of peers at or below |

---

## Quick Conversion Formulas

- **SS to T:** T = (SS − 100) × (10/15) + 50
- **SS to z:** z = (SS − 100) / 15
- **T to SS:** SS = (T − 50) × (15/10) + 100
- **Scaled to SS:** SS = (Scaled − 10) × (15/3) + 100
- **z to SS:** SS = (z × 15) + 100

## Report Score Explanation

Include in reports for non-specialist readers:

> Percentile ranks indicate the percentage of same-age individuals in the normative sample who scored at or below the patient's level. For example, a score at the 25th percentile means the patient performed as well as or better than 25% of same-age peers. Scores between the 25th and 75th percentile are considered within the Average range.
