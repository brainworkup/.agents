# Cognitive Impact of Depression

> Depression can produce cognitive deficits that mimic neurocognitive disorder. Understanding the cognitive profile of depression is essential for differential diagnosis in neuropsychological evaluation.

## Cognitive Effects of Depression

- **Psychomotor slowing** (processing speed)
- **Attention/concentration** difficulties
- **Working memory** weakness
- **Executive dysfunction** (planning, initiation)
- **Subjective memory complaints** (often > objective deficits)
- **Pseudodementia** presentation in older adults

---

## Depression vs. Neurocognitive Disorder

| Feature | Depression | NCD |
|---------|-----------|-----|
| Effortful processing | Affected | Affected |
| Recognition memory | Intact | May be impaired |
| Response style | "I don't know" | May confabulate |
| Subjective complaints | Prominent, consistent | May minimize deficits |
| Course | Potentially reversible | Progressive (degenerative) |
| Insight | Preserved (often excessive) | May be impaired |
| Motor speed | Slowed | Variable |
| Language | Typically intact | May decline |

---

## Assessment Recommendations

- Administer mood measures (BDI-II, PHQ-9) alongside cognitive tests
- Compare subjective complaints to objective performance
- Look for "suboptimal effort" pattern that may actually reflect depression
- Consider treatment trial if diagnosis is unclear
- Re-evaluate after mood stabilization if cognitive concerns persist
