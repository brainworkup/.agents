## Report Structure Overview

| Section | Purpose | Length |
|---------|---------|--------|
| Header | Patient identification, dates, providers | Fixed format |
| Reason for Referral | Why evaluation was requested | 1–2 paragraphs |
| Background Information | Comprehensive history | 2–4 pages |
| Behavioral Observations | Patient presentation during testing | 1 page |
| Tests Administered | List of all measures | List format |
| Test Results & Interpretation | Domain-by-domain scores and interpretation | 3–6 pages |
| Summary & Diagnostic Impressions | Synthesis and diagnosis | 1–2 pages |
| Recommendations | Actionable next steps | 1–2 pages |
| Signature Block | Credentials and contact | Fixed format |

## Header Format

```
CONFIDENTIAL NEUROPSYCHOLOGICAL EVALUATION

Patient Name:          [Last, First]
Date of Birth:         [MM/DD/YYYY]
Age at Evaluation:     [X years, Y months]
Date(s) of Evaluation: [MM/DD/YYYY]
Date of Report:        [MM/DD/YYYY]
Referring Provider:    [Name, credentials]
Evaluating Clinician:  [Name, credentials]
```

## Core Writing Principles

### Tone and Voice
- **Third person, past tense** — Evaluation already occurred
- **Professional, clinical, objective** — Avoid colloquialisms
- **Positive but neutral** — Avoid pejorative language
- **Person-first language** — Unless patient prefers otherwise

### Language Precision
| Avoid | Use Instead |
|-------|-------------|
| "proves" or "confirms" | "suggests" or "is consistent with" |
| "below average" | "low average range" (when SS 80–89) |
| "patient failed" | "scores fell below cutoff" |
| "deficit" (within normal limits) | "relative weakness" |

### Critical Principles

1. **Premorbid Estimation** — Anchor interpretation to estimated premorbid ability
2. **Pattern Analysis** — Look for convergent evidence across measures
3. **Base Rates** — 1–2 low scores in comprehensive battery may be normal variability
4. **Ecological Validity** — Connect test performance to real-world functioning
5. **Validity Integration** — State clearly if validity is compromised

## Section-by-Section Guidance

### Reason for Referral
**Purpose:** State why evaluation was requested, by whom, and what questions it aims to answer.

**Content:**
- Referral source and specific questions
- Patient's/family's primary concerns
- Purpose (differential diagnosis, treatment planning, accommodations, forensic, disability)
- Brief description of presenting problems

---

### Background Information

Integrate history from clinical interview, collateral sources, records, and prior evaluations.

**Subsections:**
1. **Developmental and Medical History** — Birth, milestones, conditions, injuries, medications
2. **Psychiatric and Emotional History** — Diagnoses, treatments, current symptoms, trauma
3. **Family History** — Psychiatric, neurological, learning conditions in relatives
4. **Educational History** — Level, special services, accommodations, performance
5. **Occupational/Vocational History** — Employment, job performance, military service
6. **Social and Functional History** — Living situation, ADLs/IADLs, driving, social functioning

---

### Behavioral Observations

Document patient presentation during testing. Provides validity context.

**Include:**
- General appearance, orientation
- Motor observations (gait, tremor, lateralized findings)
- Speech and language (rate, rhythm, fluency)
- Mood and affect (congruence, range)
- Effort and engagement
- Test-taking behavior
- Rapport and interpersonal style
- Sensory accommodations

**Validity Statement:**
```
Based on behavioral observations and formal performance validity testing,
the current results are considered a valid and reliable estimate of
current neurocognitive functioning.
```

---

### Test Results and Interpretation

Present results domain-by-domain with interpretation anchored to normative classifications.

**Domains:**
- Intellectual Functioning
- Attention and Executive Function
- Learning and Memory
- Language
- Visuospatial/Visuoconstructional
- Motor Functioning
- Emotional/Behavioral
- Adaptive Functioning (if administered)

See [[score-interpretation-reference]] for detailed classification guidance.

---

### Summary and Diagnostic Impressions

**Structure:**
1. **Opening summary** — Restate referral and demographics
2. **Cognitive profile summary** — Strengths, weaknesses, pattern
3. **Integration with history** — Cognitive findings related to medical/psychiatric history
4. **Diagnostic formulation** — Primary and differential diagnoses with DSM-5-TR/ICD-10 codes
5. **Functional implications** — Impact on daily living, school, work, social functioning
6. **Validity and limitations** — Factors qualifying interpretation

See [[diagnostic-frameworks-overview]] for diagnostic criteria.

---

### Recommendations

Provide **specific, actionable, numbered** recommendations tailored to the individual.

**Categories:**
- Medical/Neurological (referrals, imaging, labs, medication)
- Psychological/Psychiatric (therapy, medication consultation)
- Cognitive Rehabilitation (therapy, external aids, environmental modifications)
- Educational (IEP/504, accommodations, tutoring)
- Vocational/Occupational (rehabilitation, workplace accommodations)
- Safety/Functional (driving evaluation, capacity, supervision)
- Lifestyle (exercise, sleep, nutrition)

## Common Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| **Over-pathologizing** | Consider premorbid level; Average scores are not weaknesses |
| **Under-contextualizing** | Always connect scores to functional implications |
| **Boilerplate recommendations** | Tailor recommendations to the individual |
| **Missing integration** | Explain patterns, not just list scores |
| **Ignoring validity** | State limitations when validity is compromised |
