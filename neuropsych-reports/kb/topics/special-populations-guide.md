## Pediatric Evaluations

### Expanded Developmental History
- Pregnancy and birth complications
- Achievement of developmental milestones
- Early intervention services
- Daycare/preschool concerns

### Teacher/School Input
- Teacher rating scales (BRIEF, Conners, BASC)
- Classroom observations
- Educational records review (report cards, IEPs, standardized testing)

### Additional Measures
- Vineland-3 or ABAS-3 (adaptive behavior)
- ADOS-2, ADI-R (autism assessment)
- Educational achievement testing

### Recommendations Focus
- IEP/504 plan recommendations
- Specific accommodations (extended time, preferential seating)
- Specialized instruction (Orton-Gillingham for dyslexia)
- Grade retention/advancement considerations
- Transition planning (for adolescents)

## Geriatric/Dementia Evaluations

### Collateral Interview (Essential)
- Informant relationship and reliability
- Onset and progression of cognitive changes
- Comparison to prior functioning
- ADL/IADL changes (driving, finances, medications)
- Behavioral changes (personality, apathy, disinhibition)

### Functional Assessment
- Instrumental ADLs (finances, medications, transportation)
- Basic ADLs (grooming, dressing, toileting)
- Safety concerns (wandering, falls)

### Capacity Considerations
- Medical decision-making capacity
- Financial capacity
- Testamentary capacity (if requested)
- Driving capacity

### Staging
- CDR (Clinical Dementia Rating)
- GDS (Global Deterioration Scale)
- FAST (Functional Assessment Staging)

### Differential Diagnosis Focus
- MCI vs. Major NCD
- Alzheimer's, vascular, Lewy body, frontotemporal
- Depression-related cognitive difficulties (pseudodementia)

## Forensic Evaluations

### Role Clarification (Critical)
- **Treating clinician** — duty to patient
- **Independent examiner** — retained by one party
- **Court-appointed expert** — neutral evaluator

### Enhanced Validity Requirements
- Performance validity testing (PVT) — mandatory
- Symptom validity testing (SVT) — mandatory
- Consider malingering base rates in litigating populations
- Document consistency of presentation with known patterns

### Daubert/Frye Standards
- Use validated, peer-reviewed tests
- Apply published cutoffs for validity measures
- Base conclusions on established diagnostic criteria
- Acknowledge limitations and alternative explanations

### Third-Party Observers
- APA/NAN position: should NOT be present
- If mandated by court, document potential validity impact
- Audio/video recording has similar concerns

### Functional Capacity Statements
For disability/IME evaluations, explicitly state:
- Capacity to work (what type, what restrictions)
- Capacity for independent living
- Need for supervision or assistance

### Recommendations Categories
- Medical/Neurological follow-up
- Psychiatric treatment
- Cognitive rehabilitation
- Vocational rehabilitation
- Safety/Capacity considerations

## Re-evaluation/Follow-up

### Reliable Change Methodology
- Use Reliable Change Index (RCI) thresholds
- Account for practice effects
- Document base rate of change in healthy populations

### Comparison Approach
- Baseline vs. current scores side-by-side
- Note domains of stability, improvement, or decline
- Clinical significance (not just statistical)
