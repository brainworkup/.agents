## Neurocognitive Disorders (NCD)

### Major Neurocognitive Disorder

**Criteria:**
A. Evidence of **significant** cognitive decline from previous level in one or more domains based on:
   1. Concern from individual, informant, or clinician; AND
   2. **Substantial** impairment documented by testing (typically ≥ 2 SD below norms)

B. Cognitive deficits **interfere with independence** in everyday activities

C. Not exclusively in context of delirium

D. Not better explained by another mental disorder

**Specify etiology:** Alzheimer's, frontotemporal, Lewy body, vascular, TBI, substance-induced, HIV, prion, Parkinson's, Huntington's, other/unspecified

**Specify:** With or without behavioral disturbance

---

### Mild Neurocognitive Disorder

**Criteria:**
A. Evidence of **modest** cognitive decline based on:
   1. Concern from individual, informant, or clinician; AND
   2. **Modest** impairment documented by testing (typically 1–2 SD below norms)

B. Cognitive deficits **do NOT interfere with independence** (compensatory strategies may be needed)

C. Not exclusively in context of delirium

D. Not better explained by another mental disorder

---

### Etiological Profiles

| Etiology | Typical Cognitive Profile | Key Features |
|----------|--------------------------|--------------|
| **Alzheimer's** | Amnesia (encoding deficit), then language, visuospatial, executive | Insidious onset, gradual progression; hippocampal atrophy |
| **Vascular** | Executive dysfunction, processing speed; variable memory | Stepwise or fluctuating course; white matter disease |
| **Lewy Body** | Visuospatial, attention fluctuations, executive | Visual hallucinations, parkinsonism, REM sleep behavior disorder |
| **Frontotemporal (bvFTD)** | Executive dysfunction, behavioral disinhibition | Personality change, apathy, loss of empathy |
| **Frontotemporal (PPA)** | Language predominant | Progressive aphasia; behavior/memory preserved early |
| **TBI** | Attention, processing speed, executive, memory | Clear temporal relationship to injury |
| **Parkinson's** | Executive, visuospatial, processing speed | Motor symptoms precede cognitive decline |

## Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder

### DSM-5-TR Criteria

A. Persistent pattern of inattention and/or hyperactivity-impulsivity interfering with functioning

**Inattention (≥ 6 symptoms for children; ≥ 5 for age 17+):**
1. Careless mistakes; fails to give close attention
2. Difficulty sustaining attention
3. Does not seem to listen when spoken to directly
4. Does not follow through/fails to finish
5. Difficulty organizing
6. Avoids tasks requiring sustained mental effort
7. Loses things necessary for tasks
8. Easily distracted
9. Forgetful in daily activities

**Hyperactivity-Impulsivity (≥ 6 for children; ≥ 5 for age 17+):**
1. Fidgets or squirms
2. Leaves seat when remaining seated expected
3. Runs/climbs inappropriately (restlessness in adults)
4. Unable to engage in leisure quietly
5. "On the go" or "driven by a motor"
6. Talks excessively
7. Blurts out answers
8. Difficulty waiting turn
9. Interrupts or intrudes

B. Several symptoms present **prior to age 12**
C. Present in **two or more settings**
D. Clear evidence of functional interference
E. Not better explained by another mental disorder

**Presentations:** Combined, Predominantly Inattentive, Predominantly Hyperactive-Impulsive

## Specific Learning Disorder

### DSM-5-TR Criteria

A. Difficulties learning and using academic skills, with at least one symptom persisting ≥ 6 months despite intervention:
1. Inaccurate or slow word reading
2. Difficulty understanding what is read
3. Difficulties with spelling
4. Difficulties with written expression
5. Difficulties mastering number sense or calculation
6. Difficulties with mathematical reasoning

B. Skills substantially below age expectations with significant interference

C. Onset during school-age years

D. Not better accounted for by ID, sensory deficits, other disorders, or lack of instruction

**Specify:** Reading (dyslexia), Written Expression, Mathematics (dyscalculia)
**Specify severity:** Mild, Moderate, Severe

---

## Intellectual Disability

### DSM-5-TR Criteria (Intellectual Developmental Disorder)

A. Deficits in intellectual functions (IQ approximately ≤ 70 ± 5)

B. Deficits in adaptive functioning limiting functioning in daily life

C. Onset during developmental period

**Severity based on adaptive functioning:**
- Mild, Moderate, Severe, Profound

---

## Autism Spectrum Disorder

### DSM-5-TR Criteria

A. Persistent deficits in social communication and interaction:
1. Deficits in social-emotional reciprocity
2. Deficits in nonverbal communicative behaviors
3. Deficits in developing/maintaining relationships

B. Restricted, repetitive patterns of behavior, interests, or activities (≥ 2):
1. Stereotyped movements, use of objects, or speech
2. Insistence on sameness, inflexible routines
3. Highly restricted, fixated interests
4. Hyper- or hyporeactivity to sensory input

C. Symptoms present in early developmental period
D. Symptoms cause clinically significant impairment
E. Not better explained by intellectual disability

**Specify severity:** Level 1–3 for social communication and restricted/repetitive behaviors
