# Neuropsychological Evaluation Report — Quality Checklist

Use before sign-out, dictation finalization, or sharing outside the care team. Complement with institutional QA and legal review for forensic cases.

---

## Referral and structure

- [ ] Referral question(s) stated clearly at the outset
- [ ] Report sections follow local standard or logical flow (history → procedures → findings → integration → recommendations)
- [ ] Conclusions explicitly **answer** the referral question(s)

---

## Identifiers and consent

- [ ] Headers and body contain only **approved identifiers** for the intended audience
- [ ] For teaching or public drafts: **de-identified** per policy; consider running `clinical-reports/scripts/check_deidentification.py`
- [ ] Consent / release / ROI documented as required for recipients of the report

---

## History and records

- [ ] Relevant medical, neurological, psychiatric, developmental, educational, and social history included as appropriate
- [ ] **Records reviewed** listed; missing records noted when relevant
- [ ] Prior testing summarized and integrated (not ignored if available)

---

## Observations and conditions

- [ ] Behavioral observations address appearance, engagement, language, sensory/motor needs, fatigue, emotional presentation
- [ ] **Testing conditions** and session structure (e.g., split dates) noted when they affect interpretation

---

## Procedures and documentation

- [ ] Tests listed with **edition/form** and **language** of administration
- [ ] **Normative reference** (e.g., age/education as per manual) indicated where scores are reported
- [ ] Procedures beyond testing (interview, collateral) described

---

## Validity

- [ ] Performance validity and symptom validity addressed when measures were administered or when data **require** discussion
- [ ] Impact of validity concerns on **specific domains** or conclusions stated
- [ ] Wording professional and non-stigmatizing

---

## Interpretation

- [ ] Findings integrated by **domain** and linked to **everyday function** where appropriate
- [ ] Avoid over-reading **single subtests** or isolated low scores without convergent evidence
- [ ] **Premorbid** or baseline context considered when inferring change
- [ ] **Cultural, linguistic, and norm-fit** limitations noted when applicable

---

## Diagnosis and integration

- [ ] Differential reasoning present when clinically appropriate
- [ ] Diagnostic labels (ICD-10 / DSM-5) consistent with local practice and **supported** by text
- [ ] Mood/psychiatric findings distinguished from **cognitive** findings when both exist

---

## Recommendations

- [ ] Recommendations are **specific**, **feasible**, and tied to findings (not generic boilerplate)
- [ ] School/work/medical referrals name **purpose** of referral
- [ ] Accommodations or strategies are **concrete** (who implements, in what setting)

---

## Limitations

- [ ] Limitations section present and includes norms, cooperation, sensory, language, comorbidities, records, or validity as appropriate
- [ ] Scope boundaries clear (especially if not treating clinician)

---

## Forensic addendum (if used)

- [ ] Purpose, scope, and role stated
- [ ] Materials reviewed and questions addressed listed
- [ ] **Fact vs opinion** distinguished
- [ ] Alternative hypotheses and limits of opinion included
- [ ] Reviewed by retaining professional / counsel when required

---

## Visuals and attachments

- [ ] At least one **figure, profile summary graphic, or table** aids integration (e.g., domain profile, timeline)—per **clinical-neuropsych-reports** SKILL.md
- [ ] Figures de-identified; consent for any identifiable images

---

## Final read

- [ ] Spelling of names, dates, test names, and scores verified against source data
- [ ] Credentials, contact, and signature blocks complete
