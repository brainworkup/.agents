# MMPI-3 and PAI Interpretation

> T-score interpretive ranges for the Minnesota Multiphasic Personality Inventory-3 (MMPI-3) and Personality Assessment Inventory (PAI), the two most common broadband personality measures in neuropsychological evaluation.

## MMPI-3 T-Score Ranges

| T-Score Range | Interpretation |
|--------------|----------------|
| < 39 | Low |
| 39–64 | Average / Within Normal Limits |
| 65–79 | Elevated (Clinically Significant) |
| ≥ 80 | Very Elevated |

**Key Validity Scales:** F, Fp, Fs, FBS-r, RBS, TRIN, VRIN

---

## PAI T-Score Ranges

| T-Score Range | Interpretation |
|--------------|----------------|
| < 59 | Within Normal Limits |
| 60–69 | Moderate Elevation |
| 70–79 | Marked Elevation |
| ≥ 80 | Extreme Elevation |

**Key Validity Scales:** NIM, PIM, MAL, DEF, RDF, INC, INF

---

## BASC-3 / BRIEF-2 T-Score Ranges

| T-Score Range | Interpretation |
|--------------|----------------|
| < 60 | Within Normal Limits |
| 60–64 | At-Risk / Borderline |
| ≥ 65 | Clinically Significant |

---

## Interpretive Notes

- Always check **validity scales first** before interpreting clinical scales
- Elevated validity scales may invalidate the entire profile
- Consider medical/neurological contributions to personality scale elevations
- TBI patients may show elevated somatic scales (FBS-r on MMPI, SOM on PAI)
- Forensic contexts: report specific scale elevations with cutoffs and references
