# Test Results and Interpretation

> Presenting neuropsychological test scores domain-by-domain with clinical interpretation anchored to normative classifications.

## Structure

Present results by cognitive domain:
1. Intellectual Functioning
2. Attention and Executive Function
3. Learning and Memory
4. Language
5. Visuospatial / Visuoconstructional
6. Motor Functioning
7. Emotional / Behavioral (if administered)
8. Adaptive Functioning (if administered)

---

## For Each Domain

1. **Tests administered** with versions
2. **Normative classification**
3. **Clinical interpretation** — what the score means functionally
4. **Pattern analysis** — how it relates to other domains
5. **Contextual factors** — premorbid, effort, cultural/linguistic

---

## Score Table Format

```
| Measure | Subtest/Index | Score | Percentile | Classification |
|---------|--------------|-------|------------|----------------|
| WAIS-V  | FSIQ         | 98    | 45th       | Average        |
| WAIS-V  | VCI          | 105   | 63rd       | Average        |
```

---

## Interpretive Principles

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| Premorbid estimation | Compare current scores to estimated premorbid level |
| Pattern analysis | Look for convergent evidence across measures within domain |
| Base rates | 1–2 low scores in comprehensive battery may be normal |
| Ecological validity | Connect test performance to real-world functioning |
| Validity integration | State limitations if validity is compromised |

---

## Domain Template

```
#### [Domain Name]

[Patient]'s performance on measures of [domain] was [overall classification].
[He/She/They] obtained scores in the [classification] range on [specific test]
([score type] = [value], [percentile]th percentile), suggesting [functional
interpretation]. [Pattern analysis]. [Contextual factors].
```

---

## Score Explanation Paragraph

Include after first score table:

```
Standard scores have a mean of 100 and standard deviation of 15. Scaled
scores have a mean of 10 and SD of 3. T-scores have a mean of 50 and SD
of 10. Percentile ranks indicate the percentage of same-age peers who
scored at or below that level.
```

---

## Key Points

- Bold or flag impaired scores (≤ 5th percentile)
- Include confidence intervals for composite scores
- Connect scores to functional implications
- Note any accommodations or non-standard administration
