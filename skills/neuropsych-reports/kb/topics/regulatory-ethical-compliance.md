## HIPAA Compliance

### 18 HIPAA Identifiers (to remove for de-identification)

1. Names
2. Geographic subdivisions smaller than state
3. Dates (except year) — birth, evaluation, injury
4. Telephone numbers
5. Fax numbers
6. Email addresses
7. Social Security numbers
8. Medical record numbers
9. Health plan beneficiary numbers
10. Account numbers
11. Certificate/license numbers
12. Vehicle identifiers
13. Device identifiers
14. Web URLs
15. IP addresses
16. Biometric identifiers
17. Full-face photographs
18. Any other unique identifying characteristic

### Special Considerations for Neuropsych
- Ages over 89 → aggregate to "90 or older"
- Detailed injury narratives may be identifying
- Rare diagnoses with small populations increase re-identification risk
- School names, employer names, treating clinician names are indirect identifiers
- Legal case details may be uniquely identifying

### Minimum Necessary Standard

| Purpose | Information Included |
|---------|---------------------|
| Treatment referrals | Full report typically appropriate |
| School accommodations | Focus on cognitive profile and functional impact |
| Employer/disability | Include functional capacity; limit personal history |
| Legal/forensic | Scope defined by psycholegal question |

## APA Ethical Standards

### Standard 2.01 — Competence
- Practice within boundaries of competence
- Neuropsychological evaluation requires specialized postdoctoral training
- Board certification (ABPP/ABCN) is gold standard

### Standard 3.10 — Informed Consent
Inform patient/guardian of:
- Purpose and nature of evaluation
- Who requested evaluation
- Who will receive report
- Anticipated use of findings
- Limits of confidentiality
- Fee structure

**Forensic informed consent adds:**
- Examiner is not treatment provider
- Evaluation conducted for third party
- Reduced confidentiality protections

### Standard 9.11 — Maintaining Test Security

**Include in Reports:**
✅ Test names and versions
✅ Scores and classifications
✅ Qualitative descriptions of performance
✅ Error patterns and learning curves

**Do NOT Include:**
❌ Specific test items or prompts
❌ Verbatim responses revealing test content
❌ Test stimuli or images
❌ Scoring criteria or algorithms
❌ Copyrighted test forms

## Forensic Standards

### Daubert Standard
1. Is theory testable/falsifiable?
2. Subjected to peer review and publication?
3. Known or potential error rate?
4. Standards controlling technique's operation?
5. General acceptance in scientific community?

### Malingering Assessment
Forensic contexts require explicit address of:
- Performance validity test (PVT) results
- Symptom validity test (SVT) results
- Consistency with known neurocognitive patterns
- Consistency of reported symptoms with performance

### Third-Party Observers
- APA/NAN position: third-party observers should NOT be present during testing
- If mandated by court, document potential impact on validity

## Record Retention

### General Guidelines
- Retain records for period required by state law (typically 7 years for adults)
- Until age of majority + state retention period for minors
- Some states require longer retention

### What to Retain
- Signed informed consent
- Complete test protocols
- Raw data and scoring
- Final report
- Correspondence
- Authorization for release forms
- Billing records
- Interview notes
