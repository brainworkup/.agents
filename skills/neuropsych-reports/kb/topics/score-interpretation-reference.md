## Primary Classification Systems

### Wechsler System (Most Common)

Used for: WAIS-V, WISC-VI, WMS-IV, WIAT-4, and most SS-based measures

| Standard Score | Percentile Range | Classification |
|---------------|-----------------|----------------|
| ≥ 130 | ≥ 98th | Very Superior |
| 120–129 | 91st–97th | Superior |
| 110–119 | 75th–90th | High Average |
| 90–109 | 25th–74th | Average |
| 80–89 | 9th–24th | Low Average |
| 70–79 | 2nd–8th | Borderline |
| ≤ 69 | ≤ 1st | Extremely Low |

---

### Heaton / Neuropsychological Norms System

Used for: TMT, Grooved Pegboard, FAS, BNT (Heaton norms)

| T-Score | Classification |
|---------|---------------|
| > 59 | Above Average |
| 40–59 | Average |
| 35–39 | Low Average |
| 30–34 | Mildly Impaired |
| 25–29 | Mildly-to-Moderately Impaired |
| 20–24 | Moderately Impaired |
| < 20 | Severely Impaired |

| SS | T-Score | Scaled Score | z-Score | Percentile |
|----|---------|-------------|---------|------------|
| 145 | 80 | 19 | +3.0 | 99.9 |
| 130 | 70 | 16 | +2.0 | 98 |
| 120 | 63 | 14 | +1.33 | 91 |
| 110 | 57 | 12 | +0.67 | 75 |
| 100 | 50 | 10 | 0.0 | 50 |
| 90 | 43 | 8 | -0.67 | 25 |
| 80 | 37 | 6 | -1.33 | 9 |
| 70 | 30 | 4 | -2.0 | 2 |
| 55 | 20 | 1 | -3.0 | 0.1 |

**Conversions:**
- SS = (z × 15) + 100
- T = (z × 10) + 50
- Scaled = (z × 3) + 10

## Base Rates of Low Scores

Understanding expected low scores in healthy populations:

| Battery Length | Expected Scores ≤ 5th %ile |
|---------------|---------------------------|
| 5 tests | 0–1 |
| 10 tests | 0–2 |
| 15 tests | 1–3 |
| 20 tests | 1–4 |
| 25 tests | 2–5 |
| 30+ tests | 2–6 |

**Clinical Implication:** Single low scores in comprehensive batteries may reflect normal variability. Look for convergent evidence.

## Reliable Change Index (RCI)

For serial evaluations:
- Change ≥ 1.0 SD (15 SS points) typically exceeds RCI at 90% confidence
- Consult test-specific RCI tables in test manuals
- Account for practice effects
- Regression to mean affects extreme initial scores

## Demographically Adjusted Norms

**Common sets:**
- Heaton norms (age, education, sex, race)
- NAB (age, education, sex)
- NACC UDS3 neuropsychological battery norms
- Mayo Older Americans Normative Studies (MOANS)

**When to use:**
- Questions involving decline from premorbid level
- Demographic factors differ from standardization sample
- Report both adjusted and unadjusted when correction is substantial
