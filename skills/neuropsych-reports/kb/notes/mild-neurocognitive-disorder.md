# Mild Neurocognitive Disorder

> DSM-5-TR criteria for Mild Neurocognitive Disorder (MCI), representing modest cognitive decline with preserved functional independence.

## Related Topics

## DSM-5-TR Criteria

### A. Evidence of **modest** cognitive decline from previous level:

1. Concern from individual, informant, or clinician; AND
2. **Modest** impairment documented by testing (typically 1–2 SD below norms)

### B. Cognitive deficits **do NOT interfere with independence**

Greater effort, compensatory strategies, or accommodation may be needed, but independence maintained.

### C. Not exclusively in context of delirium

### D. Not better explained by another mental disorder

---

## Key Distinction from Major NCD

| Feature | Mild NCD | Major NCD |
|---------|----------|-----------|
| Impairment level | Modest (1–2 SD) | Significant (≥ 2 SD) |
| Functional impact | Does NOT interfere with independence | Interferes with independence |
| Compensatory strategies | May need them | Often insufficient |
| ADLs | Complex IADLs may require more time/effort | Assistance needed with complex IADLs |

---

## Subtypes

- **Amnestic MCI:** Predominant memory impairment (higher progression to Alzheimer's)
- **Non-amnestic MCI:** Other domains affected (language, executive, visuospatial)
- **Single-domain vs. Multiple-domain**

## ICD-10 Coding

| Condition | ICD-10 Code |
|-----------|-------------|
| Mild NCD due to Alzheimer's | G30.9 + F06.70/71 |
| Mild NCD due to vascular | G46.8 + F06.70/71 |
| Mild NCD due to TBI | S06.- + F06.70/71 |

**F06.70** = without behavioral disturbance
**F06.71** = with behavioral disturbance

---

## Clinical Significance

- Risk factor for progression to Major NCD
- Amnestic single-domain has highest conversion rate
- Some patients remain stable or improve
- Intervention opportunity window
