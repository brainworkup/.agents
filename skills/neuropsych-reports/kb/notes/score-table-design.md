# Score Table Design for Neuropsych Reports

> Principles and formats for designing clear, professional score tables in neuropsychological evaluation reports.

## Related Topics
- [[test-results-interpretation]]
- [[score-conversion-metrics]]
- [[score-classification-wechsler]]

---

## General Guidelines

- Clear, descriptive title (e.g., "Table 1. Neuropsychological Test Results: Attention and Executive Function")
- Column headers with score metric and units (e.g., "Standard Score (M=100, SD=15)")
- Left-align measure names; right-align or center numeric scores
- Footnotes for abbreviations, norms used, and accommodations
- Consistent decimal places within each table
- Number tables sequentially if using multiple

---

## Table Formats

**By Domain (Recommended):** One table per cognitive domain within the interpretive section.

**Comprehensive Appendix:** All scores in a single table at the end, organized by domain.

**Hybrid:** Summary scores in narrative; full subtest breakdowns in appendix.

---

## Formatting Conventions

- **Bold or flag impaired scores** (≤ 5th percentile / ≤ SS 75)
- Or use asterisk (*) with footnote
- Include **95% CI** for composite/index scores
- If using multiple norm sets, use separate columns or tables with clear labels

---

## Score Explanation Template

Include after first score table:

> Standard scores have a mean of 100 and standard deviation of 15. Scaled scores have a mean of 10 and SD of 3. T-scores have a mean of 50 and SD of 10. Percentile ranks indicate the percentage of same-age peers who scored at or below that level.
