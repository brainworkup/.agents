# Demographically Adjusted Norms

> When and how to use norms adjusted for demographics (age, education, sex, race/ethnicity) in neuropsychological assessment, and their clinical implications.

## Common Norm Sets

| Norm Set | Adjustments |
|----------|-------------|
| Heaton norms | Age, education, sex, race |
| NAB | Age, education, sex |
| NACC UDS3 | Age, education |
| MOANS (Mayo) | Age-based; older adult norms |

---

## When to Use

- Referral question involves **decline from premorbid level**
- Demographic factors **significantly differ** from standardization sample
- Forensic evaluations requiring precision
- Always **report which norms were used**

---

## When to Report Both

Consider reporting both adjusted and unadjusted scores when:
- Demographic correction is substantial (e.g., large education adjustment)
- Comparing to prior evaluations using different norms
- Legal/forensic context demands transparency

---

## Caveats

- Demographically adjusted norms may **obscure true impairment** in groups with historically lower educational access
- Adjustment may create an artificially high baseline, making decline harder to detect
- Clinical judgment should integrate normative data with individual context
- Different norm sets may produce different classifications for the same raw score
