# APA Ethical Standards for Assessment

> Key APA ethical standards (Standards 2.01, 3.10, 9.01–9.11) applied specifically to neuropsychological assessment practice.

## Standard 2.01 — Competence

- Practice within boundaries based on education, training, supervised experience
- Neuropsychological evaluation requires specialized postdoctoral training
- Board certification (ABPP/ABCN) is gold standard but not legally required in most states

---

## Standard 3.10 — Informed Consent

Before evaluation, inform patient/guardian of:
- Purpose and nature of evaluation
- Who requested it and who receives the report
- Anticipated use of findings
- Limits of confidentiality
- Whether this is a treatment relationship
- Right to decline (if not court-ordered)
- Fee structure

**Forensic additions:** Not treatment provider; evaluation for third party; reduced confidentiality protections

---

## Standard 9.01 — Bases for Assessments

- Base opinions on sufficient information and appropriate techniques
- Do not diagnose from insufficient data
- If evaluation is limited, state limitations clearly

---

## Standard 9.02 — Use of Assessments

- Use tests appropriate to purpose and population
- Consider validity and reliability for the specific patient
- Account for linguistic, cultural, and individual differences
- Do not base conclusions on obsolete instruments

---

## Standard 9.04 — Release of Test Data

**Test Data** (scores, responses) ≠ **Test Materials** (manuals, items, stimuli):

- Test data may be released to patient or designee with valid authorization
- Test materials should NOT be released to non-psychologists
- In legal proceedings, advocate for protective orders for test materials

---

## Standard 9.06 — Interpreting Results

Account for: purpose, test factors, test-taking abilities, situational/personal/linguistic/cultural differences

---

## Standard 9.11 — Maintaining Test Security

- Do NOT include test items, stimuli, or specific prompts in reports
- DO include: test names, scores, classifications, qualitative descriptions
