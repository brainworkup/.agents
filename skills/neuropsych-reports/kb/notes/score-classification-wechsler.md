# Wechsler Score Classifications

> Standard score classification system used for WAIS-V, WISC-VI, WMS-IV, WIAT-4, and most norm-referenced measures.

## Classification Table

| Standard Score | Percentile Range | Classification |
|---------------|-----------------|----------------|
| ≥ 130 | ≥ 98th | Very Superior |
| 120–129 | 91st–97th | Superior |
| 110–119 | 75th–90th | High Average |
| 90–109 | 25th–74th | Average |
| 80–89 | 9th–24th | Low Average |
| 70–79 | 2nd–8th | Borderline |
| ≤ 69 | ≤ 1st | Extremely Low |

---

## Score Metrics

- **Mean:** 100
- **Standard Deviation:** 15
- **Range:** Typically 40–160

---

## Applied to Tests

| Test | Applications |
|------|---------------|
| WAIS-V | Adult intellectual functioning |
| WISC-VI | Child/adolescent intellectual |
| WPPSI-IV | Preschool/pediatric intellectual |
| WMS-IV | Memory functioning |
| WIAT-4 | Academic achievement |

---

## Score Distribution

- **68%** of population falls within Average (90–109)
- **95%** falls within 70–130
- **2.5%** falls below 70
- **2.5%** falls above 130

---

## Clinical Interpretation

- Low Average (80–89) is **not** impaired — still within normal limits
- Borderline (70–79) is low but may not indicate significant impairment without other evidence
- Compare to estimated premorbid level for decline assessment
- Consider base rates — low scores occur in healthy populations
