# Test Security in Neuropsychological Reports

> Guidelines for what to include and what to avoid in neuropsychological reports to maintain test security and comply with copyright and ethical standards.

## Related Topics
- [[apa-ethical-standards-assessment]]
- [[hipaa-compliance-neuropsych]]
- [[forensic-evaluation-standards]]

---

## What to Include ✅

- Test names and versions
- Standard scores, scaled scores, T-scores, percentiles
- Normative classifications (e.g., "Average," "Impaired")
- Qualitative descriptions of performance patterns
- Error types and categories (e.g., "intrusion errors," "perseverative responses")
- Learning curves and retention rates (e.g., "recalled 12/16 words after delay")
- General descriptions (e.g., "difficulty with cognitive flexibility")

---

## What NOT to Include ❌

- Specific test items or prompts (e.g., "When asked to define 'sanctuary'...")
- Verbatim patient responses that reveal test content
- Test stimuli or images
- Scoring criteria or algorithms
- Specific items missed (e.g., "could not name 'asparagus' on BNT")
- Reproduction of copyrighted test forms

---

## Gray Areas ⚠️

| Content | Guidance |
|---------|----------|
| Clock Drawing Test reproductions | Generally acceptable if commonly published |
| Rey Complex Figure Test copy | Figure is widely published; reproduction in reports generally acceptable |
| CVLT-3 word lists | Do NOT include; describe patterns instead (e.g., "semantic clustering") |

---

## Legal Proceedings

- Advocate for protective orders to prevent public disclosure of test materials
- Separate test data (releasable) from test materials (protected)
- Object to deposition questions that require revealing test items
