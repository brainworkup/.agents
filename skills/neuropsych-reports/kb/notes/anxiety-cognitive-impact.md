# Cognitive Impact of Anxiety

> Anxiety can produce cognitive deficits particularly in attention, working memory, and processing speed. Understanding anxiety's effects helps differentiate from primary neurocognitive impairment.

## Cognitive Effects of Anxiety

- **Attention/concentration** difficulties (internal distraction from worry)
- **Working memory** interference (cognitive resources consumed by anxious thoughts)
- **Processing speed** reduction (overthinking, second-guessing)
- **Test anxiety** effects (underperformance on timed measures)
- **Subjective cognitive complaints** disproportionate to objective findings

---

## Anxiety vs. NCD Pattern

| Feature | Anxiety | NCD |
|---------|---------|-----|
| Attention | Fluctuating, worse when anxious | Consistently impaired |
| Working memory | Worse under stress | Consistently impaired |
| Timed tests | Variable (anxiety-dependent) | Consistently slow |
| Recognition memory | Intact | May be impaired |
| Complaints | Excessive worry about cognition | May minimize deficits |
| Physical symptoms | Somatic anxiety features | Less prominent |

---

## Assessment Considerations

- Administer anxiety measures (BAI, GAD-7) alongside cognitive tests
- Test anxiety may inflate processing speed deficits — consider behavioral observations
- High-anxiety patients may perform below ability on timed tasks
- Consider administering untimed alternatives when anxiety is prominent
- Note: Anxiety and genuine NCD can coexist — don't dismiss real cognitive impairment
