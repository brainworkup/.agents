# Intellectual Disability (ID) Criteria

> DSM-5-TR diagnostic criteria for Intellectual Disability (Intellectual Developmental Disorder), emphasizing deficits in intellectual functioning and adaptive behavior with onset during the developmental period.

## DSM-5-TR Criteria

**A.** Deficits in intellectual functions confirmed by clinical assessment AND standardized testing (IQ ≈ ≤ 70 ± 5, considering SEM)

**B.** Deficits in adaptive functioning limiting one or more daily activities across multiple environments:
- Communication
- Social participation
- Independent living

**C.** Onset during the developmental period

---

## Severity Levels (Based on Adaptive Functioning, Not IQ)

| Level | DSM-5-TR | ICD-10 | Description |
|-------|----------|--------|-------------|
| Mild | 317 | F70 | Can live independently with support |
| Moderate | 318.0 | F71 | Requires ongoing support |
| Severe | 318.1 | F72 | Requires extensive support |
| Profound | 318.2 | F73 | Requires pervasive support |

---

## Assessment Considerations

- Severity is determined by **adaptive functioning**, not IQ alone
- Use both intellectual testing (WAIS-V, WISC-VI) and adaptive measures (Vineland-3, ABAS-3)
- Consider cultural and linguistic factors in IQ interpretation
- Rule out sensory impairments, lack of educational opportunity, and language barriers
- IQ score is not a fixed label — consider confidence intervals (±5–7 points)
- Co-occurrence with ASD is common — assess both independently
